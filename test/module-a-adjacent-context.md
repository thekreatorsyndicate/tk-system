# Calvin Cycle And Light Dependency

The Calvin cycle happens in the stroma of the chloroplast.

It does not directly require light, but it depends on ATP and NADPH made during the light-dependent reactions.

This is why the Calvin cycle is indirectly dependent on light even though light is not one of its direct reactants.

The light-dependent reactions happen in the thylakoid membranes and produce oxygen as a byproduct.
